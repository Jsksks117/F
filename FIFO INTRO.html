FIFO Page Replacement Algorithm

First-In, First-Out (FIFO) page replacement algorithm offers a straightforward approach to paging. When a page fault occurs, signifying a requested page is not currently in memory, FIFO prioritizes evicting the oldest loaded page. This eviction strategy adheres to a queueing system, where the page loaded earliest resides at the front and is subsequently the first to be replaced.While FIFO boasts advantages in terms of implementation simplicity and low overhead due to its clear logic, it can be susceptible to Belady's anomaly. This anomaly highlights scenarios where increasing available memory frames can counterintuitively lead to a rise in page faults. Additionally, FIFO makes no distinction regarding a page's recent usage patterns, potentially removing pages that may be needed soon.Therefore, FIFO is best suited for workloads exhibiting strong temporal locality, where recently used pages are likely to be accessed again in the near future.

Advantages: Simple to implement, low overhead.
Disadvantages: Poor performance under random access and working set models. Does not consider access locality, leading to unnecessary page evictions.
