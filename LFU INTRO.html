LFU Page Replacement Algorithm

LFU, or Least Frequently Used, focuses on preserving pages with the lowest frequency of use. This algorithm keeps a count of the number of times each page is referenced. When a page fault occurs and replacement is necessary, LFU selects the page with the lowest reference count. The underlying assumption is that pages with infrequent access are less likely to be in demand shortly.

Advantages:Can be beneficial for workloads with a mix of frequently and infrequently accessed pages. Less overhead compared to LRU as it doesn't require tracking access recency.
Disadvantages: Might not perform well under access patterns with strong locality of reference, potentially evicting relevant pages within a working set. May not be suitable for workloads where the most recently accessed pages are the most important ones
