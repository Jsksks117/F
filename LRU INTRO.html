LRU Page Replacement Algorithm

LRU, or Least Recently Used, is a sophisticated algorithm that prioritizes retaining the most recently accessed pages in the memory. It functions on the premise that pages not accessed for an extended period are less likely to be needed in the near future. LRU maintains a queue or a linked list of pages, and when a page replacement is necessary, it selects the page that has not been accessed for the longest duration, ensuring that recently used pages remain readily available in the memory.

Advantages: Generally good performance across various access patterns, especially for workloads with locality of reference. Relatively simple to understand and implement.
Disadvantages: Requires maintaining timestamps or access counters for all pages, which can add overhead compared to FIFO. Might not be optimal for workloads with frequent context switching or unpredictable access patterns
